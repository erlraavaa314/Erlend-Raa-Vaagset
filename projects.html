Academic Projects

My current focus is on broadening the scope of my research and teaching — especially in the areas of democratic education and robotics. I'm particularly interested in how educational environments can be more participatory, student-driven, and responsive, both in theory and in practice.

On the robotics side, I’m beginning to explore path planning problems for agricultural robots — including raspberry-picking systems. It’s an early-stage inquiry, but an exciting intersection of algorithms and real-world complexity.

I also supervise a range of student projects, particularly in AI and web development. These include work on large language models (LLMs), retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), image recognition, time series analysis, and more. Through this, I’m constantly learning — both from the technologies and from the students shaping them. For examples of recent work, see the mentoring page.

Alongside new directions, I'm also revisiting and wrapping up several long-running collaborations and unpublished results. These include:

  • Ongoing work on persistent homology computations with Nello
  • Research on embeddings of simplicial complexes with Mitchel and Amir
  • Joint work on discrete Morse theory with Geevarghese Philip
  • Member of a pedagogical research group at HVL with Bjarte and Marcin, focused on in-class video use and related themes

Teaching & Outreach Initiatives

I co-organize our local Kattis programming competitions, which we run in a social-learning format at the Læringslabben (our student learning lab at HVL). The event runs alongside math drop-in sessions — and yes, there are always fresh waffles.

The sessions are open to HVL students and local high schoolers, and are co-hosted with Gizem and Camilla. Our goal is to make algorithmic problem-solving accessible, informal, and community-driven — part competition, part curiosity, part comfort food.

I also volunteer at community tech events like the Global Game Jam, and I'm working on launching a local branch of SciSnack — a writing and discussion group for students and researchers interested in science communication, reflection, and informal knowledge sharing. The initiative is part of an international network of the same name.
