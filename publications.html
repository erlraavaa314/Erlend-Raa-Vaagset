Publications

Here's a list of my publications. If you want the details, check out the links. Or just send me a message if you have any questions.

  • PhD Thesis: "Optimal Parameterized Algorithms for Solving NP-Hard Problems in Topology"

    This thesis combines parameterized complexity theory with computational topology, focusing on the development of FPT algorithms for fundamental problems like homology localization, minimum bounded chains, and subsurface recognition. The work introduces new techniques such as WiPS reductions, establishes several hardness results, and proves that some algorithms are optimal under the Exponential Time Hypothesis (ETH), showcasing the potential of parameterized techniques in tackling complex topological problems. It also contains several new results not contained in the papers listed below.

    University of Bergen, 2024. DOI Link

  • "ETH-Tight Algorithms for Finding Surfaces in Simplicial Complexes of Bounded Treewidth"

    This paper investigates the parameterized complexity of finding surfaces in simplicial complexes, introducing novel FPT algorithms based on treewidth for various variants of the subsurface recognition problem. It establishes ETH-lower bounds, proving the optimality of the designed algorithms under a common hardness hypothesis, and it demonstrates the applicability of the WiPS reduction technique.

    Symposium on Computational Geometry (SoCG), 2022. DOI Link

  • "The Parameterized Complexity of Finding Minimum Bounded Chains"

    This paper delves into the parameterized complexity of finding minimum bounded chains, a problem with roots in both optimization and topology. It explores the effect of various parameters, such as solution size, coface degree and treewidth. It also gives an FPT algorithm using coface degree and solution size and improves on previously known hardness results.

    Computational Geometry, 2024. DOI Link

  • "Homology Localization Through the Looking-Glass of Parameterized Complexity Theory"

    This paper introduces the framework of parameterized complexity to the problem of finding optimal geometric representatives of homology classes. It establishes new hardness results and develops FPT algorithms using treewidth. This provides new insights for studying the complexity of problems in algebraic topology and demonstrates the power of parameterization techniques.

    Preprint, arXiv, 2020. Preprint Link

  • "Linear MIM-Width of Trees"

    We provided a linearithmic algorithm for computing the linear maximum induced matching width of a tree and an optimal layout

    International Workshop on Graph-Theoretic Concepts in Computer Science (WG), 2019. DOI Link

  • Master's Thesis: "Log Hochschild Homology of Categorical Products and Coproducts"

    This one is public but not a publication. My MSc thesis examines log Hochschild homology in commutative pre-log algebras. Key contributions include proving the commutativity of log Hochschild homology with categorical products, formulating a Künneth Theorem for log Hochschild homology, and deriving methods for computing homology in algebraic structures built through products and coproducts.

    University of Bergen, Spring 2017.

    Available at: BORA (UiB Repository)
